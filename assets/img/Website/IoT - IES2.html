Introduction

There are many variables that influence the quality and quantity of the yield in the grape farming process. In order to maximize the quality and quantity of the yield, these variables are currently monitored manually, which is time-consuming. This process must be less labour intensive.

Why Grapes?

Grape industry consists mainly of wine production (which makes around 70% of industry revenue) and table/dried grape production. This multibillion dollar industry mainly relies on grape quality and quantity which are highly sensitive to environmental factors such as temperature and water stress. Without monitoring and high level quality control, possible losses in this industry are often so high they can put smaller and larger farmers out of business. Current methods which rely on manual monitoring are time consuming and often unsustainable because they rely on human estimation of crop’s health.

What is IoT?

The internet of things, or IoT, is a system of interrelated computing devices, mechanical and digital machines, objects, animals or people that are provided with unique identifiers (UIDs) and the ability to transfer data over a network without requiring human-to-human or human-to-computer interaction.

Why IoT?

Agriculture and farming are generally very unpredictable human activities because of their dependency on weather and environmental conditions. This illustrates a need for large scale agriculture-related sensor or automation technologies paired with data analytics to help inform farmers about the condition and risks their farms are in so that they can take proper action to ensure the growth of their livestock and or crops.

What we offer



Detect, identify and appropriately notify the user of a harmful condition present on the vines



Measure and predict grape quality and yield of the vineyard



Detect and notify the user of sensor node failures (faulty data, connectivity loss)

Functions

Data acquisition, Data processing, Communication data
Data acquisition
The data acquisition should accurately and at right moment measure all the measurands. Then the measurement data has to be communicated to a central node where the processing is done. This is a separate high-level function. This communication protocol should be redundant and has to be selfaware of any errors made in transmission. Besides that the acquisition should be low power and protected against the environment.
Data processing
The data from the sensors should be combined with data from outside sources and processed into useful information for the farmer. The system should also be able to detect and correct for any failures in the system.
Communication data
The system needs to communicate different data sets and predictions to the farmer in different ways. This top-level function deviates in sub-sets of what data needs to be communicated to the farmer and in what way to make it an easy to understand system.
